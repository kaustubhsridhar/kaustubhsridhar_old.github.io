About

Hi! My name is Kaustubh Sridhar and I'm a first year PhD at Electrical and Systems Enginnering, University of Pennsylvania. I am passionate about research in Robotics with a particular interest in the safety, security and reliability of cyber-physical systems.

I am currently working with Prof. Insup Lee & Prof. Oleg Sokolysky on buidling a universal template for checkpointing and attack resilient-control of cyber-physical systems

I worked with Prof. Srikant Sukumar during my Bachelor's Thesis on Event Triggered Control of Quadrotors. We published a paper on this at the 5th CEAS Conference on Guidance, Navigation and Control, 2019

I spent the summer of 2018 at Duke University (Electrical and Computer Engineering) under the guidance of Prof. Miroslav Pajic creating a self-driving car like platform on which Intrusion Detection Systems can be tested.

In the summer of 2017, I worked on Bio-inspired Landing of Quadrotors using a Vision-INS Navigation System. This was done at the Indian Institute of Science, Banglore under the guidance of Prof. Radhakant Padhi and a paper on the same was published in the proceedings of the 5th IFAC Conference on Advances in Control and Optimisation of Dynamic Systems, 2018

I have also spent some time working on Autonomous Indoor Navigation for ground and aerial vehicles with Prof. Hemendra Arya at IIT Bombay

Details about the research mentioned above can be found here
